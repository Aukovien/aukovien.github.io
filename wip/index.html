// CURIOUS HOW IT WORKS.

Welcome to my digital portfolio! My name is Anar, and I am a passionate computer science student with a deep specialization in cybersecurity and software development. My journey into tech has been driven by an intense curiosity about how systems work, and more importantly, how they can be secured or exploited. I actively engage in offensive security research, which has shaped my mindset as a problem solver who looks for unconventional solutions to complex technical challenges.

What makes me unique is my blend of low-level security knowledge and full-stack development skills. I don't just participate in the infosec community; I actively contribute to it by developing my own Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges, such as Copycat and Fractured Prophecy. Beyond security, I have strong hands-on experience in web and backend development using modern frameworks and languages like React and Go. I also enjoy the physical side of computing, frequently building and optimizing custom PC hardware.

The purpose of this digital portfolio is to provide a comprehensive view of my capabilities that goes beyond a traditional one-page resume. It serves as a central hub where visitors can explore my technical projects, understand my approach to offensive security, and see the tangible results of my academic and extracurricular efforts.

On this site you will find my professional Resume/CV, my leadership in the CU Cybersecurity Club, highlights from major competitions like CCDC and CPTC, and deep dives into the software and CTF environments I have engineered. I am currently an undergraduate at CU Boulder, working toward a career in Security Engineering. Thank you for visiting, feel free to connect with me. I promise I won't bite.

To expand my backend system programming skills, I built bubbl, a custom CLI application designed to log and track Spotify listening history to a playlist, with duplicate detection and sync capabilities. I developed this using Go (Golang) to leverage its strong concurrency model and efficient performance. Building this required navigating the Spotify OAuth API, handling data streams securely, and structuring persistent information robustly.
